Empire State Building with Lincoln Logs

I first started out this assignment by looking for as many detailed building dimensions for the Empire State Building as I could. After searching the internet for several hours, I still was only able to find relatively obscure architechtural blueprints.

Here are some of the sites I found and referenced the most.
esbnyc.com
datavizblog.com
wikipedia.org

Using the assortment of Lincoln Log sizes, which I found at knex.com, I approximate I can use 128 logs to make a square foot. The total floor area of the Empire State Building not including the ground floor is 2,684,701 sq ft. Which would mean I need 687,283,456 Lincoln Logs for the total floor area of the structure assuming the floors are at least 2 feet thick throughout. It's 1252 ft. high (not including the antenna) and 187 ft. wide according to this site, giving each side an approximate area of 233,750 sq ft. Therefore, I would need 239,360,000 Lincoln Logs, assuming the walls are 2 feet thick(thinkness would certainly change through the building to keep structural integrity but I was unable to find any facts relating to wall thickness), to construct all four sides of the building. A rough estimate of the number of logs needed for the entire project would then be 926,643,456. Also, as a fun fact, the average price of an individual Lincoln Log is $.42 giving us $389,190,251.52 as a very rough estimate for the price of the construction materials. I'm sure, however, that we could work out some sort of bulk discount with Knex.
